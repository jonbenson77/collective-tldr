Karpathy's Auto Research Loop, Built as a Claude Skill

Andrej Karpathy (one of the original OpenAI founders) published a research-loop method that trains AI to grade itself and keep only the best version. Ben turned it into a skill anyone can install. You give it the rules ("subject line must be under 50 characters, no em dash, must use PAS or AIDA"), point it at your past performance data, and it runs 5-10 versions of itself, scores each one, throws out the losers, keeps the winners. Out of the box, his LinkedIn skill jumped from 80% pass-rate to 100% on 2 rules. From 68% to 95% on 4 rules.

  • Real numbers from Ben's tests: LinkedIn writing skill: 80% → 100% on 2 criteria, then 68% → 95% on 4 criteria. Cloud routing skill: +9.9%.
  • 3 layers of rules to teach it: hard rules (subject under 50 chars), pattern rules (use PAS or AIDA), tone rules (sounds like you not a robot).
  • Cap iterations at 10. More than 15 makes results worse, not better.
  • Pair with Claude's scheduled tasks for a Mailvio subject-line optimizer that runs every Friday by itself.
